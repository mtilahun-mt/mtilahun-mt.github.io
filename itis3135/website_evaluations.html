Website Evaluations

Below are evaluations of two websites using the CRAP principles of design: Contrast, Repetition, Alignment, and Proximity.

Apple

  • Contrast: Apple's website makes excellent use of contrast throughout. Bold, high-resolution product images are placed against clean white or black backgrounds, making key elements immediately pop. Text is consistently dark on light backgrounds (and vice versa), ensuring strong readability and visual hierarchy that guides the eye naturally from headlines to supporting details.
  • Repetition: The site maintains a strong, consistent design language across every page. The same typeface (San Francisco/Helvetica Neue), color palette, button styles, and grid structure repeat throughout, creating a cohesive brand experience. Navigation, footer layout, and section spacing are uniform, so users always know where they are.
  • Alignment: Apple uses a strict grid-based layout with precise alignment. Text and images are aligned cleanly to an invisible grid, giving every page a sense of order and professionalism. Nothing feels arbitrarily placed — every element has a deliberate relationship with the elements around it, reinforcing clarity and elegance.
  • Proximity: Related content is grouped together effectively. Product specifications appear directly beneath the product they describe, calls-to-action sit adjacent to the features they relate to, and navigation items are logically clustered. This clear grouping reduces cognitive load and helps users find information quickly and intuitively.

Arngren.net

  • Contrast: Arngren.net fails dramatically at contrast. The page is an overwhelming sea of images, text, prices, and links all competing for attention at once, with no clear visual hierarchy. There is no dominant focal point — everything shouts at the same volume. The chaotic mix of colors, sizes, and styles makes it nearly impossible to distinguish important content from background noise.
  • Repetition: There is virtually no consistent repetition of design elements. Images vary wildly in size, fonts change unpredictably, and there is no unified style for links, buttons, or headings. Rather than creating cohesion, the site feels like hundreds of unrelated elements were pasted together without any visual plan or design standard.
  • Alignment: The site has essentially no alignment. Products and text blocks are scattered randomly across the page with no grid or logical structure. Elements overlap, crowd each other, and appear to have been placed wherever space happened to exist. This complete lack of alignment makes the page feel disorganized and is deeply disorienting for the user.
  • Proximity: Related content is not grouped together in any meaningful way. Product names, images, and prices are scattered across the page with no clear pairing, making it very difficult to associate an item with its description or cost. The absence of whitespace and logical grouping creates an oppressively dense layout that overwhelms users rather than guiding them.
