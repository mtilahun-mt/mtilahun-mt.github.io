About Our Church

History, Faith, and Our Congregation

Our Congregation

Ankise Miheret Bata Lemariam — meaning “The House of Grace for Our Lady Mary” — is an Ethiopian Orthodox Tewahedo congregation located in Charlotte, North Carolina. We serve the growing Ethiopian Orthodox community in the greater Charlotte area, providing a spiritual home where the ancient liturgical traditions of our church are celebrated and lovingly preserved.

Our congregation warmly welcomes anyone who wishes to learn about the Ethiopian Orthodox Tewahedo faith. We celebrate the Holy Qidase (Divine Liturgy) in Ge'ez and Amharic, and uphold the fasts, feasts, and sacraments of our tradition with devotion.

We are committed to nurturing the next generation of believers through Sunday School and youth programs taught in both Amharic and English, ensuring that the wisdom and beauty of our faith endures.

History of the Ethiopian Orthodox Faith

The Ethiopian Orthodox Tewahedo Church is one of the oldest Christian denominations in the world. Christianity was introduced to Ethiopia in the 4th century when Frumentius, a Syrian Christian scholar, was consecrated by St. Athanasius of Alexandria and became the first Bishop of Ethiopia. King Ezana of Axum subsequently adopted Christianity as the state religion — making Ethiopia one of the very first nations in history to do so.

The word Tewahedo (ተዋሕዶ) means “unity” or “being made one” in Ge'ez, reflecting the church's miaphysite Christological position — the belief that Jesus Christ has one united divine-human nature. This theological stance unites the Ethiopian Orthodox Church with the Coptic, Armenian, and Syriac Orthodox traditions in the Oriental Orthodox communion.

The church maintains its own ancient liturgical language, Ge'ez, used in worship alongside Amharic. The Kebra Nagast (Glory of Kings) traces the lineage of the Ethiopian royal dynasty to King Solomon and the Queen of Sheba, while the Ethiopian Orthodox biblical canon — the largest of any Christian church with 81 books — includes texts such as Enoch and Jubilees preserved in Ge'ez alone.

Over 1,600 years of continuous tradition have shaped the Ethiopian Orthodox Church into a rich tapestry of theology, sacred art, music (Zema), architecture, and spiritual practice that is truly unique in the Christian world.

Pillars of Our Faith

🙏

Prayer

Prayer is the foundation of Ethiopian Orthodox life. The faithful pray facing east at the canonical hours of the day, drawing on the ancient Psalms of David and the rich prayers of the church in Ge'ez and Amharic.

🕊

Fasting

Ethiopian Orthodox Christians observe some of the most extensive fasting practices in all of Christianity — approximately 180–250 fasting days per year, including the 55-day Lenten fast, Wednesdays, and Fridays.

☩

Tabot

The Tabot — a consecrated replica of the Ark of the Covenant — is the most sacred object in an Ethiopian Orthodox church, kept in the innermost sanctuary (Holy of Holies) of every congregation.

📜

Scripture

The Ethiopian Orthodox Church uses a canon of 81 books, the broadest of any Christian tradition, including texts such as the Book of Enoch and Jubilees, uniquely preserved in the Ge'ez language.

🎶

Liturgy

The Qidase (Divine Liturgy) follows rites attributed to the Apostle Matthew, St. Basil, and St. Cyril, celebrated in Ge'ez with the unique sacred chants (Zema) of our tradition.

🤝

Community

The Mahiber and Senbete are fellowship traditions that bring members together for worship, mutual support, and celebration of the saints' feast days throughout the year.
